Drill Reference

Pre-Session Mirror Checklist

Work through this before every session. Tap to check each point off.

Eyes over the ball
Your right eye should sit directly over the ball or just inside the line. As a right-eye dominant player, being too far inside causes you to aim right. Use the mirror to confirm your eye line is true.
Shoulder alignment
With a natural arc stroke, players commonly aim the shoulders right of target. Check that your shoulders are parallel to the target line — the mirror should show both shoulders level and square.
Face angle at address
The DS72 sight line is strong — use it as your guide. The face should be square at address. Even a slight open face at address will compound through your arc.
Ball position
Ball should be just forward of centre — roughly inside left eye. Too far forward opens the face through impact; too far back closes it.
Grip pressure
Rate it 3–4 out of 10. Light enough to feel the putter head, firm enough for control. Tight grip kills feel and promotes manipulation. Breathe out before you stroke.
Posture and stillness
Head and lower body must stay completely still through impact. The mirror will show any head lift or sway. Practise with your nose pointed at the ball until after the ball has rolled 12 inches.
Arc stroke note: Your natural arc means the face rotates open on the backswing and closes through impact — this is correct. Don't force a straight-back-straight-through path. The key is returning the face to square at impact, not eliminating the arc.
Right-eye dominant tip: Dominant eye players tend to set up slightly inside the ball. If you're consistently missing right, re-check eye position first before adjusting your aim.

60mm Gate

Warm-up

Rhythm and flow. The gate as a guide, not a test.

Gate position
Place the 60mm gate 15" in front of the ball, centred on your intended line.
Distance
Putt from 3–4ft. Close enough that the gate feels comfortable to pass through.
Reps
10–15 putts. Focus on tempo and follow-through, not gate avoidance.
Ready to move on
10 clean passes in a row → proceed to 50mm or begin your session.
The 60mm gate should feel easy. If you're clipping it regularly, the issue is tension — widen your attention to stroke rhythm rather than the gate itself. A tight grip or hesitant stroke will wander more than a free one.

50mm Gate — 15"

Calibration

Face angle check. Only 5mm clearance each side.

Gate position
Place the 50mm gate 15" in front of the ball, precisely centred on your line.
Distance
Start from 4ft. Build to 6ft once you're passing cleanly.
Reps
10–15 putts. Stop before fatigue or frustration sets in.
Benchmark
15 clean passes from 6ft = excellent session.
Clipping one side consistently?
That's a setup issue, not a stroke issue. Go to the mirror — check shoulder alignment and eye position before adjusting anything else.
The 50mm gate is a calibration tool, not a performance target. A short burst of clean passes tells you what you need to know. More reps than necessary breeds tension and a tentative stroke — the opposite of what you want on the course.

50mm Gate — 24"

Start-line test

Bigger separation reveals path issues the 15" position can mask.

Gate position
Move the 50mm gate to 24" in front of the ball — double the usual distance — centred on your line.
Distance
Putt from 4–6ft. The increased gate distance amplifies any arc drift or path deviation.
Reps
8–10 putts. Use this as a diagnostic only — not a warm-up drill.
What to look for
If you're passing 15" cleanly but clipping at 24", your start line is drifting after the gate — face is closing or opening slightly post-impact. Compare with your mirror session.
This is the toughest gate test in your kit. Passing the 50mm at 24" means your start line is genuinely consistent. Don't use this as a daily drill — save it for when you want honest feedback about where your ball is actually starting.
Gate drill order: 60mm (15") → 50mm (15") → 50mm (24") if needed. Each step adds demand. Stop when you have useful information, not when you've exhausted yourself.

Pressure Ladder — How It Works

Level 13 in a row from 3ft
Level 23 in a row from 4ft
Level 33 in a row from 5ft
Level 43 in a row from 6ft

Miss at any level → drop back one level and restart that level's streak from zero. The app tracks total putts taken and drop-backs, so you can track efficiency over time, not just completion.

Why it works: The pressure is cumulative — getting to Level 4 and missing back to Level 3 is far more stressful than starting from scratch. This mimics the feeling of a short putt on the back nine that actually matters.
Mindset cue: Before each putt, pick your spot (not the hole), commit to your line, and then let the stroke happen. The ladder rewards decision-making under pressure, not mechanical perfection.

PuttOut Perfect Putt Trainer

The trainer rewards centre-face, proper-pace putts. Offline or over-pace — it spits the ball back.

Perfect putt (3pts): The ball enters the centre and is caught by the micro-target. Requires perfect pace and a square face through impact. Aim for this — don't settle for holed.
Holed (1pt): Ball enters the trainer but doesn't hit the perfect putt zone. Good enough on a real green, but tells you that face or pace was slightly off.

Gate + PuttOut Combination

Place the 50mm or 60mm gate 15" in front of the ball. The gate tests path; the PuttOut tests pace and face.

Gate is centred on your intended line
PuttOut is 6ft from ball position
You can see the PuttOut centre through the gate
Mat is flat and stable
Pace tip: Aim to hit the PuttOut with enough pace that it would travel 12–18" past the hole on a real green. Too soft and the trainer spits it back even on a good line.
